    During the time 9th of April 1993 - 1st of August 1996 I've released 18
   collections. What can I say? It's been fucking fun to do something and at
   the same time get mail at boards saying "keep it up!" etc. That must mean
  that You appreciate what I do or? I really do hope that, and that You still
   will think like that - sure gives me the motivation to continue making it
    even if I sometimes think that it sucks etc. But one thing that really
   suck is the concept "elite board" .. Nowadays the concept is dead! Except
    for some BRAVE dudes trying to bring the concept back, ie. not allowing
   that crappy Aminet stuff online! You guys are really brave! Remember the
   first board in Sweden that did this, Flashback back in late 1994 - early
   1995 - Twister was first, and many followed him. Suddenly there popped up
       millions and millions of boards with names like "Fantasy Tower" &
   "Byte Planet" for instance. Both these boards were runned by 14 year old
    kids that probably had parents that got fed up with the bills and told
    them to do something else than call and call.. "Yes!! I open a bbs!!!"
      was the first thing they got in mind.. Blah! Hate it! Well, I don't
   complain about the fact that many new boards are kewl, but where has the
    good old names went!?! Names like "Pirates Haven", "Ice Station Zebra"
   and the former Euro-top board "Crashpoint". These boards still got their
             reputation among the older sceners, the cream of us!

                    Here is some sceneinformation from me:

         1988 began drawing gfx in DPaint. Started some groups with my
    friends, names like Deception, Hurricanes etc. were in mind. I had the
   handle Sacrilege at that time. In 1990 I joined, at that time one of the
  best demogroups in Europe, Addonic. I made some gfx for some demos. In 1992
   I left them coz of lack time, but when I discovered that I got that time
    I joined a group called Industry - damn that time was kewl! Went to the
      very kewl TCC-party in Gothenburg and we released a CHIPMUSIC-DISK!
  One of the nicest things with that party was that I actually saw one of my
                heroes Rwo/Kefrens and I also talked to him :)

   As alot of groups do, Industry died in late 1993. I left the scene for a
  while. Got back when a friend of mine bought a modem and we found out that
    we didn't have to wait for the lazy swappers to send the productions by
     mail - we got them directly from the groups BBS's instead! Suddenly I
      started "trading" around on some BBS's. While trading it was kinda
 booring to watch the NComm screen, so I loaded Cygnus Ed and made some logos
  that my friend Godhead learned me how to do :) . I released my first colly
                  and the other ones came quickly after that.

  In 1994 I got an offer to join 2000 AD - man! A major group - I joined and
   one week after I joined, some guys from a new group called Artcore got in
   touch with a friend of mine, and told him that they would like to have me
  as their 5th member in Artcore. I joined, and released the 2nd AC! release
  some days after. Continued like this until 1995, when this friend of mine,
    Stain/Classic told me that he was tired of the scene, and he would sell
    his Amiga 4000 and his BBS, Final Impact. I was fast, bought the Amiga
  and put FI online after just two weeks. Got busted for using a PBX. Closed
   the BBS for a while put it back online again with more HD and new modem.

       After a while, I was out of money - the bills growed over my head
       so I decided to close down from december -> february to get money
    for all my needs. I got back, joined the so called Dreamchart and oopps
  I was the 2nd fastest board in Sweden -> the 6th fastest board in the World
    Cheesus! After that I got unemployed and here I am, sitting like a fool
                           in front of the computer.
