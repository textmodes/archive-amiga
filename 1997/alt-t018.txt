        In 1993 I made|my very first  attempts to create|some ascii art.
        I bought my first modem during the  summer and started calling a
        couple of local PD boards.  At one of them I saw|some quite nice
        work made by a|guy who called himself Xtc. As he|also happend to
        be a fellow musician, we started - during the autumn - to talk a
        lot about  music,  the demo scene  and even  ascii.  In December
        (after he had |renamed himself  to Incognito)  he asked me  if I
        wanted to form| an ascii group  together with him  and Vermin (a
        local graphic artist who also made some ascii). |I said yes, and
        we formed  Altern.  But  pretty soon  we got bored of  ascii and
        decided to kill the group in  March 1994 before either one of us
        made a  proper| public  appearance  (my  second |collection  was
        released under|the ALT label,  but didn't use a filename stating
        that it  was an Altern  release).  I dropped  my|current  handle
        (Brutal) and decided to use my real name instead. Incognito lost
        some letters in his handle and became Inco.     |
                      |                                 |
        In 1995  I started to make  some ascii again  and joined up with
        the ascii scene for real this time. The rest is history.  During
        the autumn  last year  I got bored  with my current  ascii group
        (Epsilon Design)  and decided to leave  them in December.  I got
        loads of joining offers  from really good groups| but decided to
        say no since I|thought it wouldn't  be much of a|difference from
        E^D.  I started to think  a lot about starting  my own label.  I
        tried to come up with  some fitting names  for a|couple of weeks
        when it  suddenly  struck me:  Why don't  I get |Altern  back in
        business?     |                                 |
                      |                                 |
        So,  when I went to Inco's home on  February the|18th I told him
        that I wanted |to put  Altern  back on  the map.| He agreed  and
        decided to join up with the reborn force.       |
