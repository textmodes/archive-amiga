           chapter 1 of 3 - pressure to succeed

           I dont mean to  say that  i'm  not grateful  for all the
           kind words,  all the  ranking  places  in  compos and in
           different charts but as with  everything, there's always
           a turn side.

           drawing ascii  has always been   for fun,  and hopefully
           it'll stay that way,  atleast  for me. however, nowadays
           i feel i  always  have to   get  better  with  designing
           collections, logos and infofiles,  to meet the expectations
           of the audience, *you*.„

           after getting a  good place  in WorldCharts  (R.I.P) and
           in some  other charts  i cant  remember,  and  winning a
           few ascii  competitions, i've ran out of goals. Seems to
           me i've had everything  i've wanted from my ascii career.
           being in divine stylers, with people  i've always looked
           up to, is also  more than i  could've  wish   for. Other
           than those,  i would love to  run a group of  my own but
           with  the present  situation  there's  no  time for that
           kind of  stuff anymore.  job, studies  and a  home  with
           my girlfriend take too much of my time.

           anyways,  i was  talking  about the  pressure  .. i dont
           know if its my will to please the  crowd or my own  seek
           for  perfectionism,  but  i  want  the  collection  i am
           releasing to be perfect.            . .. .

              chapter 2 of 3 - retirement

              as usual,  every now and then  i've been thinking of
              retiring  from the ascii scene.  no big deal, i have
              a million other things to do with my  life than draw
              logos with lines or $ signs.

              what keeps me from quitting?

              what  we've  witnessed  during  the last  few  years
              has  been  great,  even  guys  with kids, houses and
              wives have made comebacks. some have been successfull
              while others have faded back to the shadows..

              maybe i'm too  scared that  when i'd be doing my own
              comeback after four years of inactivity, i would have
              nothing to come back to.  four years  is a long time,
              think of  the  difference  between 1993 and 1997 for
              an example.

              maybe  quitting just  isnt as  easy as  it  was back
              then  when you could just sell your hardware or sell
              the phoneline.  nowadays  you can  access  sal-one's
              forum and most  of the  telnet bbs's from almost any
              computer hooked on the internet.

              by 2006,  are we just  a bunch  of old  guys posting
              into  few forums  and  telling  the kids  of present
              havent got any balls to  draw decent ascii? hope not,
              if i find myself doing that, I'LL QUIT! :) ..
