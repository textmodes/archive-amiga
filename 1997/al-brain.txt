| BORING BULLSHIT                                                              |
`------------------------------------------------------------------------------'

 A while ago I never thought I'd release another collection. I was tired of
 the scene, tired of making logos, tired of computers. I decided to close down
 my BBS early last summer and sold most of my Amiga equipment. Since I couldn't
 get that much money for my plain A1200 I decided to keep it for a while.

 Later on I bought a PC, mostly for school and internet usage. My Amiga ended
 up in my closet. Later on I got an email from my friend Exocet where he told
 me he had some new collections coming up. Some other members of Arclite seemed
 to be in the same situation after being inactive for a while (except Mogue
 then who's active all the time; keep the good work up mate).

 This inspired me, and I plunged (well, more or less) into my closet and dug
 up my old Amiga. I started CygnusEd and tried to create some logos. Most of
 the logos in this collection is a result of this rather impulsive comeback.

 At the moment I'm also a frequent visitor on the IRC-channel #Ascii. There
 you can reach me if you have something on your mind or just want to chitchat
 for a while. I'm not sure this sudden comeback will last, this could be my
 last collection. But I think I've said this before ;-)

 Some guys have tried to persuade me to attend at the Remedy party. I haven't
 made up my mind yet, but since I live near the partyplace I might show up. In
 case I do, see you there. In case you'd like to contact me for some reason,
 my email address is included at the end of this collection.

 Another thing that struck me when I looked through some of the collections
 being released recently was the old-school vs. new-school topic. I thought
 this battle was over since long by now. Apparently it's not. If someone wants
 my opinion, I will share it with you right now.

 I think logos should be possible to read without somekind of translation
 character by character. Most work by so called old-school artist were in fact
 readable. Please note the word "most" in the previous sentence. There are also
 some newer artists whos styles are quite readable. But very much is in my
 opinion almost unreadable. That's a pity.

 Anyway, watch out for the official Arclite Home Page, soon to be announced!

 Before I end this I'd like to welcome our latest artist, H2o. Welcome to
 Arclite mate! I'm sure you'll feel comfortable in this group.


 // Peter (a/k/a Danzig of Arclite)
