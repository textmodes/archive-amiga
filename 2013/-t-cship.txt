thing like "Divine Stylers" become "Devainaa Sutairaasu". To make the matters
worse, they also try to make the foreign words written in katakana to be
more like how they sound when properly pronounsed. So, "super" becomes
"suupaa", "church" becomes "chaachi", "of" becomes "ofu" and "the" becomes
"ze". Yeah, let me say it out loud what you think: what the fuck!

Many Japanese spend 10 years in school learning English. The most of the time
goes into vocabulary. And that consists of remembering what those words are
in katakana. Meaning, that after school they perfectly know that "cheese" is
"chiizu". Therefore you can meet a Japanese business manager, who can barely
say "herro" and "naisu to miito chuu". Great. Anyway, if you're interested in
the topic, Internet can tell the story in a lot's better and more interesting
way than my ascii colly ever could.
