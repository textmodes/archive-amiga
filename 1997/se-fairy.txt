        a few words from TANGo just to set the mood before you embark
                   on a rather strange little journey ...

           this is not my usual respects style collection. this is
       dedicated to the guys who have inspired me to carry on drawing
        ascii in the hope that one day i will come close to being as
         good. this is not an ass licking collection with a million
          artists whose styles i honestly do like - this is a small
       handful of people who i always look forward to seeing a release
                                  from ...

      if you're not in here, it doesn't mean i think your style sucks,
       because it doesn't. i could release a collection with a tribute
       to all the artists i respect because there are so many. i have
         a great many friends (or as close to friend as you can get
        without ever having met someone :) in the ascii business, but
        these are the people who, with their work, make me strive to
       improve every time i load up cygnus ed, and who knows? one day
                   i might be half as good as one of them.

     if anyone is interested i've actually put a little work into these
      logos - it is all my own style, i haven't ripped but i have tried
           to use a little imagination for once ... surprising eh?

           to my chosen gods in the business - i salute your pants

                ·  · ··:( Champignons filled with Paella ):··  ··   ·
                .

    · It's a simple, but quite exotic. All you need is:

       · an oven
       · a frying pan
       · some oil
       · any number of BIG champignons
       · paella (readymade frozen paella usually available at supermarkets
                 or you can make your own - find the recipe somewhere)
       · some sauce you like (I use a weird selfmade orange garlic-mixture)
       · some cheese


    · Put your oven on.

    · Cut out the feet from the champignons after washing them and then
      slice them. Fry with some oil for a few minutes.

    · Take the frozen/selfmade paella and pour it on the fried champignons.
      If you have frozen paella fry it for 10-12 minutes.

    · When the paella^champignon-mix is ready pour some of your chosen
      sauce on the bottom of the champignon. Then put some paella on top
      of it and finish it with some sliced cheese (cover all the paella
      with it).

    · If your oven is now 225°C put the champignons in. The time they
      should be in the oven depends on the size of your champignons, but
      an easy way to spot when they're ready is to watch the cheese.

    · Serve with vegetables or some salad. And if you're still hungry
      you'll probable have lots of paella left so you can eat it with
      your chosen sauce.


    · That wasn't so hard, was it? (well, if you have to make the paella:)
